The challenge

Many people feel alone, stressed, or unsure where to find help.

Loneliness & stigma

It can feel hard to ask for support in a culture where mental health is still private.

Stress & anxiety

Daily pressure at work and school can leave people exhausted and isolated.

Uncertain care

Many do not know where to find affordable, trustworthy support in their region.

The Hikari approach

Light in one place for everyday support and healing.

Hikari brings together mood tracking, gentle journaling, peer support, professional care, sponsored assistance, crisis resources, and mental health education so people can take calmer steps toward recovery.

Features

Tools to help people feel held, heard, and supported.

Mood Check-In

Track your feelings with a gentle prompt and receive caring suggestions.

Write privately, reflect on progress, and create space for your thoughts.

Talk to Someone

Find Professional Help

Community Support

Hybrid model

Support first. Payment should not be the thing that stops someone from getting help.

Hikari is a hybrid social enterprise. Basic tools are free. Paid services help fund growth. Donations, grants, sponsorships, and partnerships help provide support for people who cannot afford care.
